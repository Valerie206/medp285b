How much energy does it take to power the internet?

For many, the internet is an immaterial framework that has completely revolutionized our lives: we now have information at our fingertips and can instantly connect with others across the globe. However, hidden beneath the surface is a physical energy-dependent infrastructure that powers our digital world. Every click, scroll, and action relies on data centers, power grids, and servers, which contribute to carbon emissions. Energy is required to power our devices and wireless networks, so our online actions emit a small amount of carbon dioxide. However, a significant amount of carbon is released when global use is accounted for.
