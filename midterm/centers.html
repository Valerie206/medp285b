Data centers, often considered the brains or backbone of the internet, significantly contribute to the carbon footprint. They store, process, and transmit the information that powers our digital world and keeps us all connected. In Northern Virginia alone, one of the largest data center markets, data centers consume roughly 2,553 MW of power, contributing to a total global data center consumption of 416 terawatts, which equates to about 3% of all electricity generated worldwide. With AI's growing demand and advancement, the amount of energy required to maintain these systems will continue to rise, amplifying their environmental impact.
