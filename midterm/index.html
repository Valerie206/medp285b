The Surface

How much energy does it take to power the internet?

For many, the internet is an immaterial framework that has completely revolutionized our lives: we now have information at our fingertips and can instantly connect with others across the globe. However, hidden beneath the surface is a physical energy-dependent infrastructure that powers our digital world. Every click, scroll, and action relies on data centers, power grids, and servers, all of which require a large amount of energy and contribute to carbon emissions as they power our devices and wireless networks. Although each online action may seem insignificant, it emits a small amount of carbon dioxide, which quickly adds up once you consider the entire world's online activity. Estimates suggest that the carbon footprint of our devices, the internet, and supporting systems account for 3.7% of global greenhouse gas emissions. As our dependence on the internet and its infrastructure continues to grow, we must recognize the environmental cost of our digital habits and explore ways to make these processes more sustainable.
